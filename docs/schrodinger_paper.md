# Schrödinger's 1935 Paper: Detailed Analysis

## Original Publication

**Title:** Die gegenwärtige Situation in der Quantenmechanik  
**Author:** Erwin Schrödinger  
**Year:** 1935  
**Journal:** Naturwissenschaften  
**Translation:** "The Current Situation in Quantum Mechanics"

## Historical Context

In 1935, quantum mechanics was still a young and controversial field. The Copenhagen interpretation, championed by Niels Bohr and Werner Heisenberg, suggested that quantum systems exist in superpositions until measured. Schrödinger, along with Albert Einstein, was deeply troubled by this interpretation.

Einstein had recently published the EPR paper (Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen paradox), which highlighted the "spooky action at a distance" in quantum entanglement. Schrödinger's cat thought experiment was designed to take this quantum weirdness to its logical extreme.

## The Famous Thought Experiment

### Original German Text

> "Man kann auch ganz burleske Fälle konstruieren. Eine Katze wird in eine Stahlkammer gesperrt, zusammen mit folgender Höllenmaschine (die man gegen den direkten Zugriff der Katze sichern muß): in einem Geigerschen Zählrohr befindet sich eine winzige Menge radioaktiver Substanz, so wenig, daß im Laufe einer Stunde vielleicht eines von den Atomen zerfällt, ebenso wahrscheinlich aber auch keines; geschieht es, so spricht das Zählrohr an und betätigt über ein Relais ein Hämmerchen, das ein Kölbchen mit Blausäure zertrümmert."

### English Translation

> "One can even construct quite burlesque cases. A cat is put in a steel chamber, together with the following infernal machine (which must be secured against direct interference by the cat): in a Geiger counter tube there is a tiny amount of radioactive substance, so small that perhaps in the course of an hour one of the atoms decays, but equally likely none; if it happens, the counter tube discharges and through a relay releases a hammer that shatters a small flask of hydrocyanic acid."

### The Paradoxical Result

> "Hat man dieses ganze System eine Stunde lang sich selbst überlassen, so wird man sich sagen, daß die Katze noch lebt, wenn inzwischen kein Atom zerfallen ist. Der erste Atomzerfall würde sie vergiftet haben. Die Psi-Funktion des ganzen Systems würde das so zum Ausdruck bringen, daß in ihr die lebende und die tote Katze (s.v.v.) zu gleichen Teilen gemischt oder verschmiert sind."

> Translation: "If one has left this entire system to itself for an hour, one would say that the cat is still living if no atom has decayed in the meantime. The first atomic decay would have poisoned it. The wave function of the entire system would express this by containing the living and the dead cat (pardon my words) mixed or smeared out in equal parts."

## Schrödinger's Intent

### A Critique, Not an Endorsement

Schrödinger was NOT suggesting that cats can actually be in superposition. Instead, he was using this absurd example to show what he saw as the flaws in the Copenhagen interpretation:

1. **The Measurement Problem:** When exactly does the wave function collapse?
2. **Macroscopic Superposition:** Can large objects really exist in superposition?
3. **The Role of the Observer:** Does consciousness play a role in measurement?

### His Key Insight

> "Das Typische an solchen Fällen ist, daß eine ursprünglich auf den Atombereich beschränkte Unbestimmtheit sich in grobsinnliche Unbestimmtheit umsetzt, die sich dann durch direkte Beobachtung entscheiden läßt."

> Translation: "The typical feature of such cases is that an uncertainty originally restricted to the atomic domain is transformed into a macroscopic uncertainty that can then be decided by direct observation."

## Modern Interpretations

### Copenhagen Interpretation
- The cat IS both alive and dead until observed
- The wave function collapse is instantaneous upon measurement
- No explanation for why macroscopic objects don't show quantum behavior

### Many-Worlds Interpretation (Hugh Everett, 1957)
- Both outcomes occur in separate branches of reality
- The universe splits into one where the cat is alive and one where it's dead
- No wave function collapse, just branching

### Decoherence Theory
- Interaction with environment destroys superposition
- The cat becomes entangled with its surroundings
- Superposition collapses extremely quickly for macroscopic objects

### Objective Collapse Theories
- Wave function collapse is a real physical process
- Happens spontaneously for large systems
- No need for observer or consciousness

## Real "Cat States" in Modern Physics

While no one has actually put a cat in quantum superposition, physicists have created "cat states" with:

### Photons
- Single photons in superposition of different polarization states
- Demonstrated in countless experiments
- Basis for quantum cryptography

### Ions
- Trapped ions in superposition of energy states
- Used in quantum computers
- Can maintain superposition for minutes

### Superconducting Circuits
- Quantum bits (qubits) in superposition
- Basis for many quantum computing approaches
- Can create entanglement between multiple qubits

### Mechanical Oscillators
- Tiny vibrating membranes in superposition
- Approaching macroscopic scales
- Demonstrates quantum-to-classical transition

## Educational Value

The thought experiment remains valuable because it:

1. **Illustrates Superposition:** Makes an abstract concept tangible
2. **Highlights Measurement Problem:** Shows the mystery of wave function collapse
3. **Connects Microscopic to Macroscopic:** Bridges quantum and classical physics
4. **Sparks Discussion:** Encourages thinking about interpretation of quantum mechanics

## Common Misconceptions

### Misconception 1: Schrödinger believed in zombie cats
**Reality:** He thought the idea was absurd and used it to criticize the Copenhagen interpretation.

### Misconception 2: The experiment has actually been performed
**Reality:** No one has actually done this with a real cat. It's a thought experiment.

### Misconception 3: Quantum mechanics applies to everyday objects
**Reality:** Quantum effects are negligible for macroscopic objects due to decoherence.

### Misconception 4: Consciousness causes collapse
**Reality:** Most interpretations don't require consciousness; any interaction with environment works.

Recent experiments that relate to Schrödinger's concerns:

1. **Quantum optomechanics:** Creating superposition in mechanical systems
2. **Macroscopic entanglement:** Entangling large numbers of atoms
